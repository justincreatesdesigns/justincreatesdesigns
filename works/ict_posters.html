Overview

Working in a secondary school enviroment as a IT professional, we deal with many issues such as hardware, software or whatever magical thing that may happen during the term. These posters are to counter a few of those minute problems.

Observation

A few of my tasks were related to handling students directly and there are a few common issues that my team and I face day to day. So I started oberserving closely and felt that these common issues can can be resolved just with design.

Common things are:

  1. Forcing the button locked door open and getting confused on why it doesn't open.
  2. SD Cards passed to me with no name or indication of which event it was shot for.
  3. Forms of long queues infront of the office due to them forgetting their password.
  4. Knocking on the door (+ continuously knocking it when no one opens it within a short time) even when there is a clear bell there to be pressed.

The Solution

After countless repeated instructions and epic fails, I thought why not put my skills to work and visually communicate with them instead.

From my POV, students are more keen to be attracted to pretty visuals and can understand them easily if you have the right graphic or message.

The Plan

I started mapping out my limitations first. A few of them were lack of time due to work, there is a EPSON P6000 Printer but it will be overkill just for a few small ones around the office, and coloured printer is all the way on the other side of school which can be a hassle when in need of reprinting at a moments notice.

Objectives

So I concluded that I will have to:

  1. Print in Black and White for convenience as well as accessibility.
  2. A4 paper because its everywhere in the office and can easily be replenished instead of using the poster paper rolls.
  3. Visuals that are not only appealing but can also meet the standard of conveying the message to the kids.
  4. Some form of consistent identity so that it can be easily recognized and repeated.
